Protecting Small Businesses from Modern Cyber Threats

Why Cyber Awareness Matters for Small Businesses

Small businesses are increasingly targeted by cybercriminals due to limited security resources and reduced technical protection. Human error remains one of the leading causes of cyber incidents.

The Growing Risk to SMEs

Many small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) believe they are too small to be targeted. However, attackers often view smaller organisations as easier entry points due to weaker security controls and limited staff training.

The Impact of a Cyber Attack

A single phishing email or weak password can lead to financial loss, reputational damage, operational disruption, and data breaches. For small businesses, recovery costs can be significant and sometimes business-threatening.

Human Error & Social Engineering

Cybercriminals frequently exploit trust, urgency, and lack of awareness. Training employees to recognise suspicious activity significantly reduces overall cyber risk and improves organisational resilience.

Key Risks Facing Small Businesses

📧

Phishing Emails

Fraudulent emails designed to steal credentials or financial information.

🔐

Weak Passwords

Reused or simple passwords increase the risk of unauthorised access.

💻

Ransomware

Malware that encrypts business data and demands payment.
